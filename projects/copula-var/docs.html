Copula-Tail VaR Engine

Mo Minoneshan
2026
GitHub Demo

Overview

This engine estimates portfolio tail risk by modeling each asset's marginal tail with extreme value theory and the joint dependence with a Student-t copula. It then computes Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES) by Monte Carlo simulation.

0Executive Summary

Gaussian models understate joint extreme losses. By separating marginals from dependence, this engine captures fat tails and tail co-movement that drive real portfolio drawdowns.

1Motivation

In crises, correlations rise and tails fatten simultaneously. A Student-t copula encodes tail dependence that a Gaussian copula cannot.

2Marginal Tails (EVT)

Using peaks-over-threshold, excesses over a high threshold \(u\) follow a Generalized Pareto distribution:

$$ G_{\xi,\beta}(y) = 1 - \left(1 + \frac{\xi y}{\beta}\right)^{-1/\xi}, \quad y \ge 0. $$

3Dependence (t-Copula)

The Student-t copula couples uniform margins with a correlation matrix \(R\) and degrees of freedom \(\nu\), producing symmetric tail dependence that strengthens as \(\nu\) decreases.

4VaR & Expected Shortfall

For loss \(L\) at level \(\alpha\):

$$ \mathrm{VaR}_\alpha = \inf\{\ell : P(L \le \ell) \ge \alpha\}, \qquad \mathrm{ES}_\alpha = \mathbb{E}[L \mid L \ge \mathrm{VaR}_\alpha]. $$

5Implementation

engine = CopulaVaR(nu=6)
engine.fit(returns)
risk = engine.estimate(weights, alpha=[0.95, 0.99], n_sims=100_000)
print(risk["VaR"], risk["ES"])

6Validation

Backtests use Kupiec's unconditional coverage test and Christoffersen's independence test on VaR exceptions.

7Limitations

Assumptions

EVT threshold selection and copula degrees of freedom are sensitive to the sample; estimates can be unstable for short histories.
