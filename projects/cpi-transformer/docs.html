CPI Transformer Risk Dashboard

Mo Minoneshan
2026
GitHub Demo

Overview

A transformer encoder forecasts CPI inflation across horizons, and an explainability layer attributes each forecast to its macro drivers.

1Data

Inputs include lagged CPI components, labor-market series, energy prices, and survey expectations, aligned to a monthly calendar.

2Architecture

A multi-head self-attention encoder ingests the macro sequence; attention weights are

$$ \mathrm{Attn}(Q,K,V) = \operatorname{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V. $$

3Training

model = CPITransformer(d_model=128, n_heads=8, n_layers=4)
opt = torch.optim.AdamW(model.parameters(), lr=3e-4)
for epoch in range(epochs):
    for xb, yb in loader:
        loss = F.mse_loss(model(xb), yb)
        opt.zero_grad(); loss.backward(); opt.step()

4Attribution

Forecast drivers are surfaced via attention weights and SHAP values, giving a per-forecast breakdown of which inputs mattered.

5Results

The model improves on a consensus baseline in mean absolute error at short horizons, with calibrated prediction intervals.

6Limitations

Caveats

Macro regimes shift; out-of-sample performance can degrade around structural breaks and policy changes.
